    Warrant

    Source-to-Argument Scaffold

    A warrant is what licenses the move from evidence to claim. This tool makes you build your paper in that order — evidence first, argument on top of it.

    1. Stock the evidence pool on the left with your primary and secondary sources.

    2. Draft a working thesis, then build argument blocks. Each argument needs a topic sentence, one secondary source for context, three primary sources with an explanation of how each will be used, and a closing secondary source.

    3. Watch the structure glyph beside each argument. Your claim is the lintel — it stands only when the evidence beneath it is in place.

    Everything autosaves in this browser. Use Save file to keep a copy you can reopen anywhere, and Export outline to produce the Word document you'll submit.
