Historian's Toolkit Brian A. Robinson · NCCU

Tools for reading, thinking, and situating

Sourcebook & Strata

Two browser-based tools for the history classroom. One is a workspace for reading a text closely. The other is a workspace for seeing how texts about the past have been read. Both are free, both are yours to use in your own courses, and both run entirely in your browser — nothing to install, nothing to sign up for.

The Shelf

I
Sourcebook
Source Annotator

A library for primary and secondary sources. Paste a document, then annotate it in the margin through the lenses historians use — sourcing, context, audience, language, claim, question. Secondary sources get their own lenses: thesis, evidence, dialogue, production.

  • Group sources into collections by week, theme, or unit
  • Link secondary works to the primary sources they interpret
  • Print the document with annotations as endnotes for submission

 II
Strata
Historiography Timeline

A timeline that makes historiography visible. Enter the works that shaped how a subject has been written about, group them into schools of thought, and watch the interpretive strata form across the decades — the layers laid down, hardened, and cut through by the schools that followed.

  • Opens with a full sample: the historiography of Reconstruction
  • Each work carries argument, evidence base, and context of production
  • Timeline or list view; print for a discussion handout

Before you start

Where your work lives

Both tools save automatically in your browser as you go. Your work stays only on your device — nobody else can see it, not even the instructor. Each visitor has their own private workspace at the same URL.

Keep a backup

Browser storage can be cleared — by settings, updates, or shared computers. Use the Export button often. A save dialog will open so you can choose a folder. Put it inside a cloud-synced folder like Drive, OneDrive, or iCloud and your backups sync automatically.

The exported file is the submission

Turn in the JSON file the tool exports. Your instructor can import it into their own copy of the tool and see your annotations or timeline exactly as you left them.

Best on a laptop

Sourcebook's text-selection annotation works on any device, but a laptop or tablet is more comfortable than a phone. Strata browses fine on any screen size.
